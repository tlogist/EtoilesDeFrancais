# Etoiles de Francais — User Guide

## The Big Idea

This app is built on a simple insight: **you don't learn a language by studying it — you learn by using it**, with just enough structure to avoid floundering. The 5-week curriculum gives you that structure. The AI gives you a patient partner who never gets tired of your mistakes.

The 6 modules aren't random — they map to 6 research-backed strategies for language acquisition, and they reinforce each other:

| Module | Strategy | Why It Works |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| **Vocabulary** | Spaced repetition | You forget 80% of new words within a week. SM-2 scheduling shows you each word right before you'd forget it, building long-term memory with minimal effort. |
| **Conversation** | Output practice | You can't get fluent by just listening. Speaking (even typing) forces your brain to actively construct sentences, which builds different neural pathways than passive comprehension. |
| **Grammar** | Pattern recognition | Grammar rules are boring. Memory tricks stick. Each rule is taught as a shortcut ("the ne...pas sandwich") so you internalize patterns instead of memorizing tables. |
| **Pronunciation** | Sound awareness | Your brain literally can't hear sounds it hasn't learned to distinguish. Training your ear on the 20 hardest French sounds early prevents fossilized pronunciation habits. |
| **Phrases** | Chunk learning | Native speakers don't construct sentences word-by-word — they use pre-built chunks ("du coup", "en fait", "n'importe quoi"). Learning these makes you sound natural immediately. |
| **Immersion** | Environmental exposure | Your brain needs volume. Surrounding yourself with French (podcasts, music, labels, phone language) builds passive comprehension that supports everything else. |

## How a Typical Day Works

**Time commitment: 30-45 minutes/day** (can be split across the day)

### 1. Open the Dashboard

The dashboard shows your current week, day, streak, and **Today's Plan** — a curated list of 3-4 activities. Think of it as your daily workout routine for French. The plan rotates activities across modules so you're never doing the same thing two days in a row.

### 2. Follow Today's Plan (in order)

Each activity card links to the right page. A typical day:

- **Learn 5 new words** (10 min) — Flip through flashcards, listen to pronunciation, see example sentences. You're adding ~5 words/day, ~100 over 5 weeks.
- **Conversation practice** (10 min) — Pick a topic and chat with Claude in French. Don't worry about mistakes — Claude corrects you gently and adapts to your level.
- **Grammar lesson** (10 min) — Read a rule card with its memory trick, then try the practice exercises. If the explanation doesn't click, hit "Explain Differently" and Claude tries another angle.
- **Pronunciation warm-up** (5 min) — Listen, repeat, record yourself, compare. This is your vocal warm-up.

### 3. Check Off Completed Activities

The dashboard tracks completion. Green checkmark = done for today.

### 4. Advance to the Next Day

When you've finished your activities, go to **Settings > Advance to Next Day**. This is manual on purpose — you control the pace. If you need an extra day on Week 2 material, take it.

## Module-by-Module Guide

### Vocabulary (/foundation)

**Daily use:** Learn 5 new words on weekdays, review on weekends.

- **Learn** — New words appear as flashcards. Tap to flip and reveal the English translation + pronunciation. Hit the speaker icon to hear it spoken. Use "Show Example Sentences" to see Claude generate real-world usage.
- **Review** — Words you've already seen come back on a spaced repetition schedule. Rate each card honestly:
  - **Again** — No idea. (Shows again soon)
  - **Hard** — Had to think hard. (Shows again in ~1 day)
  - **Good** — Recalled with effort. (Shows again in a few days)
  - **Easy** — Instant recall. (Shows again in a week+)
- **Quiz** — End-of-week assessment. Claude generates a 10-question quiz mixing multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank, and sentence completion.

**Key insight:** The review session is more important than learning new words. Reviewing locks words into long-term memory. Skip Learn if you must, but never skip Review.

### Conversation (/conversation)

**Daily use:** One 10-minute conversation per day.

- Pick a topic from the grid (topics unlock by week).
- Type in French — however broken. Claude responds in French and provides:
  - The conversational response (which you can listen to via the speaker icon)
  - **Corrections** below the `---` line, explaining what you got wrong
- Use **"Need a hint?"** when you're stuck — Claude suggests what to say next.
- Past conversations are saved so you can review your corrections.

**Key insight:** The difficulty auto-adjusts by week. Weeks 1-2: Claude uses simple vocabulary, always translates, and is very forgiving. Weeks 4-5: Claude speaks naturally and expects you to keep up. This gradual release of scaffolding is how real fluency develops.

**Tip:** Try to use words from your vocabulary reviews in conversation. That's the connection between modules — recognition (flashcards) becomes production (conversation).

### Grammar (/grammar)

**Daily use:** One rule per weekday (5 rules/week).

- Rules are organized by week tab. Each card has:
  - **The Shortcut** — A memorable pattern or trick (e.g., "BANGS before the noun")
  - **Explanation** — Why it works
  - **Examples** — 3 sentences showing the rule in action
- **"Explain Differently"** — If a rule doesn't click, Claude generates a fresh analogy or metaphor. Hit it multiple times for different angles.
- **Practice** — Click "Practice" to get Claude-generated fill-in-the-blank exercises for that rule.
- **Cheat Sheet** — Quick reference page listing all rules with their shortcuts. Good for a quick refresher before a conversation session.

**Key insight:** You don't need to memorize conjugation tables. The shortcuts are designed to give you a "good enough" pattern that covers 80% of situations. Perfect grammar comes from practice, not memorization.

### Pronunciation (/pronunciation)

**Daily use:** 5-minute warm-up, plus expand individual sounds when learning new ones.

- **Warm-up** — A guided 5-exercise routine covering nasal vowels, the French R, U vs OU, rhythm, and connected speech. Listen to each word, repeat it 3 times, record yourself.
- **Sound cards** — Expand any sound to see:
  - IPA notation (for reference)
  - Mouth position description (how to physically make the sound)
  - Closest English equivalent
  - Practice words with listen button
  - Record button to compare your pronunciation with the model

**Key insight:** You don't need to sound perfect. The goal is to be *understood*. Focus on the French R (most distinctive), nasal vowels (most confusing for English speakers), and U vs OU (most commonly mixed up). Everything else is polish.

### Phrases (/phrases)

**Daily use:** Learn the Phrase of the Day from the dashboard. Browse categories when you're curious.

- Phrases are organized by category: reactions, filler words, opinions, casual speech, texting abbreviations, slang, idioms.
- Each phrase shows context for when/how to use it, with a slang/formal indicator.
- **"Use in context"** — Claude generates a short dialogue showing the phrase used naturally.
- Listen to each phrase to internalize the pronunciation as a complete chunk.

**Key insight:** These are the phrases that make you sound like you've been speaking French for years. "Du coup", "en fait", "bref" are used in practically every French conversation. Sprinkle them into your conversation practice and you'll notice an immediate jump in how natural you sound.

### Immersion (/immersion)

**Daily use:** Check off activities from the daily plan. Write a journal entry every other day.

- Each day has 3-4 activities: Listen (podcast/YouTube), Speak (drills), Read (texts), Write (journal).
- Activities link to external resources (Coffee Break French, InnerFrench, Piece of French, etc.).
- **Journal** — Write a few sentences in French based on the daily prompt. Submit for correction and Claude will:
  - Tell you what you did well
  - List corrections (Original → Corrected)
  - Suggest more natural phrasing
  - Encourage you

**Key insight:** The journal is secretly the most powerful feature. Writing forces you to combine vocabulary, grammar, and phrases into original sentences. Claude's corrections are personalized feedback you can't get from any textbook. Write even if it's just 2-3 sentences.

## Week-by-Week Progression

| Week | Theme | What Unlocks | You Should Be Able To... |
|------|-------|--------------|--------------------------|
| **1** | Les Bases | Greetings, numbers, essentials, basic grammar, core sounds | Introduce yourself, say please/thank you, count to 10, understand basic sentence structure |
| **2** | Construire | Food, travel, -ER verbs, articles, filler words | Order at a cafe, ask for directions, conjugate most common verbs, use "du coup" and "en fait" |
| **3** | Raconter | Emotions, time, past tense, pronouns | Talk about yesterday, express feelings, tell a simple story, give your opinion |
| **4** | Nuancer | People, actions, future/conditional, casual speech | Make plans, express wishes, have longer conversations, text in French |
| **5** | Maitriser | Questions, connectors, subjunctive, idioms, slang | Hold a real conversation, understand natural speech, use idiomatic expressions, read simple French texts |

## Tips for Success

1. **Consistency beats intensity.** 30 minutes every day is worth more than 3 hours on Saturday. The streak counter is there for a reason.

2. **Embrace mistakes in conversation.** Claude is infinitely patient. The corrections section is where learning actually happens. If you're not making mistakes, you're not pushing yourself.

3. **Say things out loud.** Even if you're typing in the conversation module, say your responses out loud before typing them. This builds the mouth-muscle memory for pronunciation.

4. **Review > Learn.** If you only have 10 minutes, do a flashcard review session. Spaced repetition is the highest-ROI activity in the app.

5. **Use the journal.** Write something every day, even one sentence. "Aujourd'hui, je suis fatigue." counts. Building the habit matters more than the length.

6. **Don't skip ahead.** The week-based unlocking exists because each week builds on the last. If Week 2 feels hard, stay on it. There's no prize for finishing early.

7. **Change your phone to French.** Seriously. You already know where everything is. You'll passively absorb dozens of words just by using your phone normally. (The Immersion module will prompt you to do this.)

8. **Listen even when you don't understand.** Put on a French podcast while doing dishes. Your brain is pattern-matching even when you think you're not paying attention. Comprehension will click suddenly after enough exposure.
